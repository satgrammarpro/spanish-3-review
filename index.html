Grammar Rules

Gustar with Nouns

+

To talk about the things that people like, use gustar + noun.

Here's how:

  • If what is liked is singular, use the singular form gusta.
  • If what is liked is plural, use the plural form gustan.

Examples:

  • Me gusta el cereal. (I like cereal.)
  • Me gustan las uvas. (I like grapes.)
  • Te gusta la sopa. (You like soup.)
  • Te gustan los jugos. (You like juices.)

Notice that the singular and plural forms of gustar match what is liked, not the person who likes it.

Gustar with People

+

Use gusta to talk about what people like to do.

Structure: Use phrases like me gusta + infinitive.

Examples:

  • Me gusta dibujar. (I like to draw.)
  • Te gusta dibujar. (You like to draw.)
  • Le gusta dibujar. (He/She/You formal like to draw.)
  • Nos gusta dibujar. (We like to draw.)
  • Os gusta dibujar. (You all like to draw.)
  • Les gusta dibujar. (They/You all like to draw.)

When you want to emphasize or identify the person you are talking about, add a + noun/pronoun.

Examples:

  • A Sonia le gusta leer. (Sonia likes to read.)
  • A mí me gusta dibujar. (I like to draw.)
  • A ti te gusta dibujar. (You like to draw.)
  • A usted le gusta dibujar. (You formal like to draw.)
